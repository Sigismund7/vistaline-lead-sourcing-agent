## Problem Statement

Vistaline Digital is a 2-person marketing agency selling marketing/digital services to renovation contractors. Cold email is the primary client-acquisition channel. Manual sourcing of contractor owner emails currently consumes ~4 hours per 100 leads, or ~20–24 person-hours per week at current 500–600 emails/week volume. This labor cost is the binding constraint on copy iteration speed: each copy variation tested against a 500-lead batch costs ~20 person-hours of data entry before the variant can even be sent. The Python pipeline skeleton (sourcer, lead_filter, owner_researcher, csv_assembler) was built to compress that 20-hour data-entry cost to minutes of compute, enabling copy iteration cycles at decision-speed rather than labor-speed. The design question is what shape and scope of further investment makes sense given that the channel is not yet validated in renovation specifically.

## Demand Evidence

- 1 closed renovation client to date, sourced through the current manual cold-email process. Client is a ~M/year renovation business in San Antonio, sitting at the floor of the stated buyer band (K–250K/month).
- Current renovation positive-reply rate (PRR): ~1%. Below typical B2B benchmarks but founder has flagged that copy and Loom personalization have known issues that would lift this with iteration.
- Secondhand validation: founder reports knowing operators in other niches running this same playbook (Instantly + Loom video personalization + scraped owner-email lists) successfully. First-party Vistaline track record in those niches was not specified in this session — see Open Questions.
- Multi-channel cadence: founder confirmed the lead list is also actively used for cold call and LinkedIn DMs, not just email. This makes the tool's ROI multi-channel rather than single-pointed on cold email working in renovation.

## Status Quo

Current process for getting renovation contractor leads into a sent campaign:

1. Google search top results in target geography, page through business listings
2. Manually identify owner name from website, BBB listing, or direct contact page
3. Cross-check with Hunter or Snov.io (manual, not API)
4. BBB lookup for owner/operator verification
5. Capture in spreadsheet: name, email, website, phone
6. Find personalization details: "detail X on project Y" (4 words max each), sourced from contractor's Instagram or website screenshots, written by Claude
7. Upload leads + custom personalization fields into Loom-style video tool with custom backgrounds
8. Field-automap into Instantly campaign and send

Current volume: 500–600 emails/week
Current sourcing labor cost: ~4 hours per 100 leads × 5–6 batches/week = 20–24 person-hours/week
At  internal rate, this is ~K–K/year of founder time on data entry.

## Premises (locked, user-confirmed)

1. **Tool's primary value = iteration speed unlock + volume.** Manual sourcing at 4 hrs/100 means each 500-lead copy-test costs ~20 person-hours of data entry. Tool collapses that to minutes, enabling copy variations to be tested at decision-speed rather than labor-speed. Both iteration speed AND total throughput matter — speed for copy iteration cycle time, volume for statistical significance once copy is dialed in.

2. **Transferability from other niches is secondhand-observed success, not first-party validated.** Founder has observed other operators running this system well in other niches but did not share niche names, client counts, or conversion math in this session. Carried as known risk.

3. **Tool does not address copy or Loom personalization quality.** Copy and Loom issues are a parallel workstream the founder has flagged. Sourcing speed and volume cannot compensate for broken copy. Tool scope is sourcing only.

4. **Lead output has confirmed multi-channel value.** Founder defended this premise against an adversarial challenge: cold call + LinkedIn DM cadences run alongside email on the same lead list today. Tool ROI is therefore not single-pointed on cold-email-in-renovation working — channel diversification is real, not aspirational.

5. **Orchestrator architecture is locked. `run.py` stays deterministic Python — no LLM-driven branching at the orchestrator level.** Adaptability lives inside individual agents via threshold checks and early-exits based on observed metrics (e.g. sourcer.py exits early when buffered leads exceed target × 1.3; owner_researcher.py caps Phase 2 calls when Phase 1 hit rate is too low; lead_filter.py raises a market-quality warning when franchise/junk ratio is excessive). This is a hard architectural constraint, also pinned in CLAUDE.md, and is non-negotiable for this plan. No task may introduce LLM-decision-making into run.py. Plan-level enforcement: any proposed change to run.py is limited to executing the existing CLI surface or fixing demonstrated bugs, never adding decision logic.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Tighten and ship (RECOMMENDED, chosen)

Make the existing skeleton production-reliable. No new features, no new APIs, no new paid surfaces. Specifically:

- Smoke test on a small city (Orlando, count=20) end-to-end
- Fix discovered reliability issues (likely targets: JS-only contractor sites needing a Playwright fallback, BBB rate-limit handling, owner_researcher false-negative review, csv_assembler FindyMail mapping validation)
- First production run on a real city (Orlando, count=50, then 200)
- Lean on Instantly's native subject-line A/B + split-campaign uploads for body/Loom variants. Read variant performance in Instantly's UI. Track per-variant booked-call and close-rate manually in spreadsheet for the first 1–2 batches.

Effort: S, ~1 week.
Risk: Low. No new APIs, no new code paths beyond reliability fixes, leverages existing paid Instantly subscription.
Reuses: 100% of sourcer.py, lead_filter.py, owner_researcher.py, website_crawler.py, csv_assembler.py, state.py, run.py.

### Approach B: Build the full iteration engine

Approach A's tightening, plus the subagent's measurement layer:
- variants.yaml for declaring 3–5 copy variants (subject + body + Loom hook tuples)
- Batch-splitting logic in csv_assembler.py that writes variant ID into the FindyMail upload as a custom field
- Instantly API integration (pull_results.py) to pull reply / positive-reply / booked / closed data programmatically per variant ID
- analyze.py command that prints variant performance with statistical confidence intervals
- Optional Streamlit or Markdown dashboard view

Effort: M, ~3–4 weeks.
Risk: Medium. Requires new Instantly API integration (new third paid surface — needs founder approval per CLAUDE.md cost discipline). Statistical analysis correctness is a new failure mode. More surface area to debug.
Reuses: 100% existing pipeline + new measurement layer on top.

### Approach C: Validate copy first, no new engineering yet

Pause tool investment. Run one more 500-lead batch via existing manual workflow with 4 deliberate copy variants assigned at sourcing time, tracked in spreadsheet. Test threshold: any variant hits ≥2.5% PRR on ≥100 sends? If yes → green-light Approach A or B with conviction. If no → copy and/or vertical is the binding constraint, the tool was not the right investment, revisit before more engineering.

Effort: 0 weeks engineering, ~20 person-hours of manual labor (4 hrs/100 leads × 500 leads, split between two founders).
Risk: Low.
Reuses: 100% of existing pipeline (none of it gets used; deferred).

## Recommended Approach

**Approach A — Tighten and ship.**

Rationale:
- The skeleton already exists. Marginal cost to production-readiness is ~1 week. The engineering investment is sunk; not shipping it is the waste.
- Approach B is the right destination but the wrong starting point. Building a programmatic measurement layer for a channel not yet validated in renovation is the over-engineering trap. Earn the right to build B by first proving (via A) that any variant at all works.
- Approach C deliberately defers the engineering value already built and doubles down on manual labor at exactly the moment the tool is meant to escape it. The 20 person-hours are also rate-limited by what 2 founders can manually do in a week, which is slower than just shipping A.
- Instantly's native A/B testing covers the easy 80% of variant analysis (subject line tests, basic per-campaign reply rates) without duplicating Instantly's own product.
- A starts the actual learning loop in week 1. B starts the learning loop in week 4.

Specific scope for Approach A:

**Campaign size cadence (used throughout):** smoke=20, validate=50, production=500. The 500-lead production batch matches your stated weekly volume target and gives the A/B cell math headroom for the decision gate (see step 6).

**Day 0 (prerequisites, before writing any code):**
- Verify your Instantly account tier supports subject-line A/B testing. Some plan tiers don't include it; if yours doesn't, decide whether to upgrade or to use 4 split-campaigns instead (no extra cost, slightly more manual ops).
- Pin the PRR decision-gate threshold (see step 6). Default 2% if no operator data is gathered; replace with a real number from the companion assignment if available.

**Day 1–2 (smoke test):** Run `python run.py --city "Orlando" --state FL --count 20`. Read every line of the master CSV manually. Spot-check 20 random outputs against source pages. Force a crash mid-batch (Ctrl+C) and confirm `python run.py --resume <campaign-id>` cleanly continues — this verifies the state-persistence contract holds in a real run, not just the happy path.

**Day 3–5 (tightening backlog, driven by smoke-test failures, not hypotheticals):** Likely targets, but only address what actually broke:
- BBB scrape rate-limit handling (back off + retry with jitter) — almost always needed
- owner_researcher Phase 1 / Phase 2 hit-rate review (target Phase 1 ≥40%, defined below — caps paid Anthropic spend per lead)
- csv_assembler FindyMail-column validation (no nulls in required fields, email regex enforcement)
- Playwright fallback for JS-rendered contractor sites — **gated**: only add if ≥30% of smoke-test failures are JS-rendering issues. If <30%, defer to v2 and pocket the time. Adding Playwright costs ~2–3 days alone (browser binaries, sandboxing, per-site detection, timeout tuning); only worth it if the evidence demands it. **If gated in, extend timeline to ~1.5 weeks.**

**Day 6–7 (validation run):** `python run.py --city "Orlando" --state FL --count 50`. Confirms the tightening fixes hold at moderate scale before committing to a 500-lead production batch. Spot-check 20 random outputs again — at least 80% accurate owner-name attribution required to proceed (protocol defined in Success Criteria).

**Week 2 (first production batch):** Run `python run.py --city "Orlando" --state FL --count 500`. Upload to Instantly via FindyMail. Configure copy variants:
- **Option 1 (works on any Instantly tier):** 4 split-campaigns × 125 leads each, one variant per campaign. Per-cell read is clean. Single batch reaches the decision-gate denominator (≥100 sends/cell).
- **Option 2 (requires A/B-enabled Instantly tier):** 2 cells × 250 leads via Instantly's native subject A/B. Simpler ops, larger per-cell sample. Single batch reaches ≥100 sends/cell with margin.
- 4-cell × 50-lead splits from earlier drafts were math-broken (couldn't reach ≥100/cell on a single 200-lead batch); ignore that pattern.

**Tracking:** Per-variant in spreadsheet alongside Instantly UI: send count, open rate, reply rate, PRR, booked calls, closed deals. A variant cell needs ≥100 sends before it counts toward the gate.

**Decision gate (end of month 1, after 1–2 production batches):** Does any variant exceed the pinned PRR threshold (default 2%, or your operator-network-derived number) on ≥100 sends per cell?
- YES → commit to next 500-lead batch with iterated variants. Begin scoping Approach B for month 2 if cycle frequency (≥3 iterations/quarter) justifies the engineering. **Document decision in CHANGELOG.**
- NO → STOP scaling. Copy and/or vertical is the binding constraint. Do not build B. Revisit copy directly (different angle, different offer framing, or different vertical) before more sourcing investment. **Document decision in CHANGELOG.**

## Success Criteria

- **Manual spot-check of 20 random outputs confirms ≥80% accurate owner-name attribution.** *Protocol:* founder compares the scraped owner name to authoritative source — BBB listing primarily, homepage 'About' page secondarily. Counts as accurate if names match exactly or are clear nickname/formal variants (e.g. "Mike" / "Michael"). Email accuracy verified by visual confirmation that the email appears on the source page or in the BBB record.

- At least one copy variant test (4-cell × 125 leads or 2-cell × 250 leads) launched in Instantly on a 500-lead production batch with per-variant tracking spreadsheet, reaching ≥100 sends per cell before evaluation
